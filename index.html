Resume

As a sophomore pursuing dual degrees in Computer Science and Mathematics at Lake Forest College, my academic journey is fueled by a deep curiosy about technology’s power to address complex challenges through creative algorithmic design.

My research interest centers on harnessing machine learning and AI to develop models that drive impactful, data-informed decisions. I’m captivated by the art of algorithm design, inspired by how these systems can streamline processes, reveal insights, and improve outcomes across diverse fields. I’m equally intrigued by the frontier of quantum computing—its potential for groundbreaking algorithms motivates me to explore how it could amplify the power of traditional machine learning, pushing all boundaries in the space. I’m excited to delve into this dynamic intersection, where AI and quantum innovations converge to transform problem-solving and deepen our understanding of the world.

I'm also involved in a research this semester with Dr. Andrew Gard.

My project examines the "randomness" of number sequences by exploring both classical and quantum-based random number generators. I developed a quantum algorithm for generating random numbers, and using statistical analysis and machine learning, I evaluate the predictability of these sequences. The resulting model will be released as an R and Python package.
This project is set to conclude by May 2025.
