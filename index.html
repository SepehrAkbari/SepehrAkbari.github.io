about me.

I'm an undergraduate student at Lake Forest College currently pursuing a Bachelor of Arts with a double major in Computer Science and Data Science, complemented by a minor in Mathematics. My academic journey has been driven by a deep fascination with technology and its potential to solve complex problems. I’m passionate about algorithm design, machine learning, and the rigorous analytical methods that underlie data-driven research.

My current research interest centers on leveraging AI and machine learning to build models that empower data-driven decision making. I am fascinated by how these models can optimize processes, reveal hidden patterns, and ultimately improve decision outcomes across various fields. At the same time, I am captivated by the promise of quantum computing and quantum-based algorithms, particularly their potential to enhance traditional ML models by introducing new paradigms in speed and accuracy. Exploring the interplay between AI/ML and quantum technologies is an exciting frontier that I believe will redefine our computational capabilities and problem-solving approaches.

Research:

My current research project examines the "randomness" of number sequences by exploring both classical and quantum-based random number generators. I developed a quantum algorithm for generating random numbers, and using statistical analysis and machine learning, I evaluate the predictability of these sequences. The resulting model will be released as an R and Python package.

This project is supervised by Dr. Andrew Gard and is set to conclude by May 2025.

papers.

Measuring the Efficiency of Binary Search Trees[Fall 2022]

This research specifically looks into two different binary search trees; the Splay Tree and the Adelson-Velskii and Landis Tree (AVL), and will compare their time complexity for the insertion of a given set of values. The term "time complexity" describes how long an algorithm takes to execute given a collection of input values of a specific size. Consequently, this research will explore “How the re-balancing algorithm efficiency of a Splay Tree compares to that of an Adelson-Velskii and Landis Tree in terms of time complexity upon insertion of values”.

Modeling a Chicken Egg[Fall 2022]

This investigation finds an appropriate mathematical model for a chicken egg and compare them. Furthermore, I will calculate the volume of the egg, and the amount of eggshell on a chicken egg or its surface area.
